Understanding Natural Disasters

üåä Tsunami

A tsunami is a series of ocean waves caused by underwater earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or landslides. These waves can travel at speeds of up to 800 kilometers per hour in the open ocean.

Warning Signs & Symptoms:

  • Rapid and unusual ocean water recession
  • Loud roaring sound from the ocean
  • Strong earthquake near the coast
  • Unusual ocean behavior
  • Water wall approaching the shore

Necessary Steps:

  • Move immediately to higher ground
  • Follow evacuation routes and signs
  • Stay away from the beach and waterfront
  • Listen to official instructions
  • Keep emergency kit ready

üèöÔ∏è Earthquake

An earthquake is the sudden shaking of the ground caused by the movement of tectonic plates beneath Earth's surface. The intensity can vary from mild tremors to devastating shocks.

Warning Signs & Symptoms:

  • Ground shaking or rolling
  • Objects swaying or falling
  • Loud rumbling sounds
  • Cracks appearing in walls
  • Animals behaving strangely

Necessary Steps:

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On
  • Stay away from windows and exterior walls
  • If outside, find an open area
  • Be prepared for aftershocks
  • Check for injuries and damage

üíß Flood

Floods occur when water overflows onto normally dry land. They can be caused by heavy rainfall, storm surge, melting snow, or dam failures. Flash floods can develop within minutes.

Warning Signs & Symptoms:

  • Heavy rainfall for extended periods
  • Rising water levels in rivers
  • Water entering buildings
  • Muddy or brown water
  • Flood warnings from authorities

Necessary Steps:

  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Avoid walking or driving through flood waters
  • Follow evacuation orders
  • Turn off utilities if instructed
  • Keep emergency supplies ready
